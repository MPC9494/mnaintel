M&A Intelligence is a weekly briefing on mergers and acquisitions — written from the buy side, for anyone who needs to understand how deals actually get priced, structured, and closed.

What it is

Buy-side intelligence.
No mandate. No fee.

Most M&A content is written by advisors with something to sell, or by journalists who have never sat in a deal room. M&A Intelligence is neither. It is written by a buy-side analyst — someone who models targets, runs due diligence, and structures transactions — for an audience that deserves the same information.

Every issue covers one real deal or one core concept. The format is direct: what happened, how it was priced, what you can take from it. No paywall on the core content. No filler. No sponsored posts.

Who reads it

Three types of reader.
One shared problem.

Business owners thinking about an exit — whether in 12 months or five years. The earlier you understand how a buyer models your business, the more time you have to close the gap between what you think it is worth and what they will pay.

Finance professionals and analysts who want the buy-side perspective that most training programmes skip. Valuation in practice looks different from valuation in a textbook, and the difference shows up in every deal negotiation.

Students and early-career professionals in M&A, investment banking, or private equity who want real deal mechanics, not theory. The case studies, issues, and tools on this site are built from actual transaction experience.
