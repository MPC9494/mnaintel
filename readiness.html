Exit Readiness Test

Is your business ready to sell?

5 questions. 3 minutes. A score that tells you exactly what is lowering your multiple right now — and what to fix before you go to market.

Question 1 of 5
What percentage of your revenue comes from your top 3 customers?
Customer concentration is one of the first things a buyer measures. High concentration means high risk — and a lower multiple.
Question 2 of 5
Could your business run without you for 6 months?
Management dependency is the second most common reason deals fail or get repriced. If you are the business, the buyer is not buying a business — they are buying a job.
Question 3 of 5
Are your financial statements audited or reviewed by an external accountant?
Clean, verified financials are essential for any serious due diligence process. Buyers who find surprises in the numbers do not renegotiate — they walk.
Question 4 of 5
What proportion of your revenue is recurring or contract-based?
Recurring revenue is the single biggest driver of premium multiples. It reduces risk for the buyer and increases their confidence in future cash flows.
Question 5 of 5
Have you had significant one-off events in the last 3 years that affected your P&L?
One-off items — restructuring costs, legal disputes, unusual revenues — need to be documented and explained. Undocumented anomalies create uncertainty, and uncertainty kills multiples.
