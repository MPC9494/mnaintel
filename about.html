About
The information gap in SME M&A.

Buyers acquire businesses for a living. Most founders sell once. That asymmetry produces a predictable outcome: money left on the table, not because the business is bad, but because the founder never understood how it was being priced on the other side.

The buy-side analyst runs the same mental model on every target. Normalised EBITDA, sector multiple, net debt bridge. The founder sitting across the table has never seen that model. M&A Intelligence exists to change that.

Not as a broker. Not as an advisor with a mandate. As an analyst who writes down what he actually sees.

The editor.
  • Currently working in M&A and Business Development at an international operating group, covering buy-side transactions, joint ventures, and debt financings across Southern Europe, DACH, and Latin America.
  • Previously Financial Advisory at Deloitte Luxembourg, leading Vendor Due Diligence mandates and raising over €200M across fundraising assignments for financial institutions.
  • Before that, Deal Advisory at KPMG Milan, focused on Distressed M&A, restructuring, and buy-side due diligence on UTP portfolios.
  • Passed CFA Level II.
Who this is for.
  • Business owners thinking about an exit, in one year or five. This is the manual that should have been available from day one.
  • Finance professionals, consultants, and advisors who want the buy-side perspective that most courses skip entirely.
  • Anyone at the start of a career in M&A, investment banking, or private equity who wants to understand how deals actually get priced, structured, and closed.
